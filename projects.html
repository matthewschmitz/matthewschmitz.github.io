Basketball Simulation Game 11/16

To practice for my first term C++ course when I was in the Systems Design Engineering course, I developed a command line basketball simulation game making use of class based design. The game places you in a late game situation of a close game and has you take control of your team. By instructing your players, whose abilities vary, to pass or shoot, you may attempt to overcome the opponent, reclaiming the lead by climbing back from a deficit, or holding a lead to claim the victory when the time expires. NBA Draft Pick Analysis Report 06/16

I conducted a statistical investigation of top 5 NBA Draft picks from 1985-1994 using Microsoft Excel features like macros and pivot tables, and statistical methods such as linear regression and standard deviation. The report was conducted in attempt to determine if a large discrepancy exists between the statistical and career achievements of first overall picks and fifth overall picks. Based upon the hypothesis that first overall picks do not perform significantly better over the course of their careers than slightly lower picks, the report set out to determine if first overall NBA Draft picks were over valued and over rated, and if the act of tanking is ultimately worth it.
